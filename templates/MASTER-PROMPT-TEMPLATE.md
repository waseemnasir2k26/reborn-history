> This is the canonical skeleton that every generated master prompt MUST follow. The META-PROMPT fills these placeholders based on niche analysis. Use this as a reference for what "good" output looks like, and as the structural contract for the generator.

> Placeholders are wrapped in `{curly_braces}`. Square brackets like `[TITLE]` are kept as-is in the final output (they are user-facing inputs, not generator placeholders).

## STRUCTURAL CONTRACT

Any master prompt produced by `META-PROMPT.md` MUST satisfy:

1. **All 26+ sections present**, in the order shown above.
2. **No `{placeholders}` remaining** in the final output — every brace must be filled with niche-specific content.
3. **Variation engine** has at least 4 axes × 5 variants.
4. **Stage progression** has at least 6 stages.
5. **Audio system** maps SFX/music/ambience to every stage range.
6. **Script Writing System** is present with explicit MODE (full-narration / minimal-narration / text-only / silent), full register lock, hook formula breaking down first 8 seconds, retention mechanics list, cue notation reference, closing-line formula, anti-fail list, and a script output format example.
7. **Lighting system** progresses across stages (never static).
8. **Failsafe** has 8+ niche-specific rules.
9. **Style Lock** specifies focal length, grade, and shadow/highlight tones.

If a generated prompt fails any of these, the META-PROMPT must regenerate the failing section before emitting.

The Script Writing System section is **never omitted** — even for silent niches it appears with `MODE: silent` and a clear "VO forbidden, on-screen text rules apply" body.
